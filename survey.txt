The Gamified IMS Awareness training is a proposed system that gamifies the current IMS Awareness training of AWS, Inc. The system is a web application created using a MERN stack.
Following the tech stack, the platform makes use of MongoDB as its centralized database, Express JS for middleware routing, ReactJS for the frontend, and NodeJS for the backend.
A simplified login and auth system is used with bcryptjs as the encryption scheme for password security. The purpose of the gamified system is to introduce a new method of enhancing awareness 
among employees regarding IMS awareness, company standards, and proper actions. 

The main feature is the training module that introduces the different IMS Awareness topics. The module includes provision of the IMS reference manual and policy videos that encompasses details of a topic. 
Each topic has a corresponding mandatory question that needs to be answered to deem a topic complete. Upon answering a mandatory question correctly, users will be awarded XP and a badge. To finish the annual IMS 
awareness training, users must simply complete all topics.

The whole gamified concept starts with enhanced user engagement. Leaning more towards a non-traditinal method, the gamified platform offers features similar to gamified applications. 
Experience Points (XP) and a leveling scheme introduces competence among users. It's easy. Earn rewards while learning. The philosophy of achieving something can help motivate users to engage with the game in intended ways.
The platform also structures the user's journey. 

According to Satoru Iwata, "...games are meant to be just one thing: Fun!"

Incorporating interactivity in learning is generally more effective than passive learning. The system adds active engagement that fosters better understanding of the topic and long-term retention.
